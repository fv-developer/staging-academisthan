Scoring Rules & Evidence Guide

Curated verbatim from: UGC Gazette 13-06-2013 (2nd Amendment, Notes 1–5 & cl. 6.0.2) and the UGC-2018-pattern CAS PBAS proforma (as adopted by University of Mumbai, GR 08-03-2019).

1 · Administrative positions — two regimes, two methods

  • UGC 2018 (Table 1, §2-A) — activity count. Holding a listed position (Head, Chairperson, Dean, Director, Convenor, Coordinator, Warden, etc.) during the assessment period = one activity. Evidence: appointment/office order with designation and From–To dates (Annexure 2-A). Concurrent or successive roles count individually. Activities across families 2-A to 2-G total into the grade: ≥3 Good · 1–2 Satisfactory · 0 Not satisfactory.
  • UGC 2013 (Category II, 6-A) — hours, not position. Only duties "that require regular office hours for its discharge" (Dean, Principal, Chairperson, Convenor, Teacher-in-charge or similar). Unit: actual hours spent per academic year, score = hours ÷ 10, combined with 6-B committee hours, capped at 30. The post itself carries no fixed points — time on task does.

2 · Hours-based items (2013 Categories I & II)

  • Compute hours from the official time-table or actual hours spent in the previous academic year; the institution verifies from the Time Table and attendance records. Only working days/weeks count.
  • Gazette worked example: 20 hrs/week × 16 weeks = 320 hrs allocated (+2 hrs/week above norm = 32 hrs excess) + 275 hrs actually taught = 627 hrs → 62.7 points.
  • Excess beyond a sub-total cap is credited at the maximum — e.g., 900 scripts (300 hrs) + 40 hrs duty = 340 hrs = 34 points, but the award is the sub-total maximum of 20.
  • Script evaluation is normed at a maximum of 20 minutes per full script (≈3 scripts/hour).

3 · Committee-graded items (2013: 4-A, 4-B, 5-A, 5-B, 5-C, 7)

  • The teacher submits evidence of work done and self-proposes a band; the screening committee finalizes the grade: Outstanding 10 · Very good 7 · Good 5 · Average 3 · Modest 1.
  • Institutions "may further develop these criteria and specify the requirements" — this is the lawful hook for university-specific rubrics (see Rubric Studio).
  • 4-C exception: proof of administering an anonymous student-feedback questionnaire earns the points (2/course, max 10) irrespective of the feedback's content; comments may not be used against the teacher.

4 · Publications, joint work & counting-once rules

  • 2013 split: first/Principal author and corresponding author/supervisor/mentor share 60% equally; all other authors share the remaining 40% equally.
  • 2018 split: two authors — 70% each; more than two — First/Principal/Corresponding 70%, each other joint author 30%. Joint supervision: 70% each. Joint projects: PI and Co-PI 50% each.
  • A paper presented at a conference and published as full proceedings is claimed once — under publications (2013 III-A / 2018 Cat 1), not under presentations.
  • Impact factor is determined per the Thomson Reuters (Clarivate) list.

5 · Caps, screening role & verification chain

  • 2013 sub-category caps as % of total API claimed: III-A 30 · III-B 25 · III-C 20 · III-D 10 · III-E 15. 2018: combined 5(b)+Category 6 ≤ 30% of the research score; score must come from ≥3 of 6 categories.
  • Gazette proviso: API scores are used for screening only and have no bearing on the expert assessment of candidates.
  • Verification chain: self-assessment on objectively verifiable criteria → institutional verification (Time Table, attendance, annexure evidence) → IQAC → Screening-cum-Evaluation / Selection Committee, whose figures prevail.

6 · What universities may customize

  • May: devise their own PBAS proforma; increase minimum required scores; add screening criteria; detail activities and adjust internal weightages within Categories I & II without changing category minimum totals (2013). Under 2018, adopt and adapt through university statutes / state GRs.
  • May not (2013, cl. 6.0.2): change the categories or scores of the API given in Appendix-III — i.e., Category III point values are fixed; minima may only move upward.
